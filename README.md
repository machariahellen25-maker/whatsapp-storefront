# WhatsApp Storefront

Small shops already sell through WhatsApp.

A customer asks about a product, gets a few photos and details, and the sale happens in chat.

This project keeps that same behavior, but makes the product side clearer before the conversation even starts.

No checkout pages. No accounts. No extra steps.  
Just products and a direct path into WhatsApp.


Most of the time, the problem isn’t the product. It’s how it shows up.

Messages get buried. Details are repeated. Customers are left scrolling or asking again.

This setup tries to clean that part up.

A product is shown once, described simply, and linked straight to chat.

If someone is interested, they don’t look for a cart. They just message.


# How it works

- a product is shown with a short description  
- a WhatsApp link opens a pre-filled message  
- the buyer sends it  
- the seller responds the same way they already do  

Nothing new to learn. Just a clearer starting point.


# What it’s for

Works best for small sellers who already rely on WhatsApp:

- jewelry sellers  
- food vendors  
- clothing resellers  
- handmade or custom products  

It doesn’t try to replace how they work. It just removes the confusion before the chat begins.

# What it feels like in use

Someone sees a jewelry piece.

They don’t scroll through a catalog or fill a form. They read a short line about it, tap once, and a message opens:

“Hi, I’m interested in this piece. Is it still available?”

From there, it’s just a normal conversation.
